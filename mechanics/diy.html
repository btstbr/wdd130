What maintenance can be done at home?

So that your car is always up to date with maintenance, it is not enough to take it to the mechanic periodically. It is also necessary to have certain daily care. Below, we give you some tips on what you can do in your garage, just with a few specific tools.

Fluids

Fluids should be checked at least once a week, or before hitting the road, always with the car cold. Note that the coolant is at the correct level, as marked on the reservoir. If it is a little lower, complete with the solution specified in the owner's manual.

For engine oil, remove the dipstick and wipe it with a cloth. See that there is no debris. Put it back and pull it out to check the level. If it's a little lower, it's normal. Top up with the same type of lubricant that is in the engine.

Windshield Wiper

The windshield wipers dry out and become brittle, especially after a while without use. Therefore, you need to change them every six months on average. For this, there are no secrets: buy the model compatible with your vehicle and follow the instructions on the package. Be careful with the wiper arm not to let go and hit the windshield.

Oil and Filters

After warming up the engine and turning it off, remove the crankcase bolt and let the old oil drain into a container. Save it for disposal at your supplier or gas station. Close the reservoir.

Remove the old filter with a filter wrench. Lubricate the new part and put it in place. Afterwards, fill the reservoir with new lubricating oil, in the quantity and specification indicated in the manual.

With the air filter, the process is simpler. The reservoir is closed only by latches or screws. Remove the old one and put the new one in its place. Clean the inside of the reservoir with a damp cloth.

Tires

Inspect the tires whenever there is an impact on them or when you feel the car is “pulling”. If a tire is flat or damaged, replace it with a spare tire and buy a new one.

To replace, loosen the screws by unscrewing them crosswise. Raise the vehicle with the jack, observing the indication on the chassis. Finish removing the screws. Replace the tire with the spare tire and screw it on. Lower the car to the ground and tighten the screws crosswise again.

Battery

If your car won't start and your battery is more than two years old, it's probably past its expiration date. It is worth remembering that the expiry date varies between manufacturers, if it is expired, it must be replaced. To change it, first unscrew the screw that holds it with an L-shaped wrench indicated in the manual. Identify the negative pole (there is a marking on the battery). Release the cable. Then release the positive cable. When installing the new battery, do the reverse, starting with the positive.
