What are the necessary basic knowledge about car mechanics?

It's true that a car is a complex machine, full of tiny parts with weird names. However, the logic of its operation is simple, as well as the engineering solutions to certain problems that existed in the early days of the automotive industry. Therefore, understanding the basics of vehicle mechanics is not difficult. See, below, some explanations and learn how the main systems of a car operate.

ENGINE

Basically, the engine is a metallic block where the cylinders are located and, inside these, the pistons. With each explosion of the air/fuel mixture inside a cylinder, the piston moves, turning the crankshaft, which, in turn, turns the flywheel.

CLUTCH

Formed by a spring-loaded housing and a friction disc pressed to the engine flywheel, the clutch system connects it to the gearbox. When it is triggered, the spring pulls the disc, which moves away from the flywheel, cutting off the transmission of engine rotation. This allows you to shift gears without any jerks.

GEARBOX

The gearbox is a set of gears of different sizes, responsible for transforming engine speed into more torque on the semi-shafts (lower gears). Also transform into more speed (higher gears) or even reverse its movement (reverse gear).

TIRES AND WHEELS

Tires are the rubber coating that is attached to metallic wheels, also called rims. The wheels are attached to wheel hubs, which receive rotation from CV joints. Between the wheel and the hub there is also the brake disc.

BRAKES

The disc brake set works with a caliper that holds the disc by means of pads. In this way, the movement of the wheels by friction is reduced. Drum brakes work by pressing shoes and linings against the inside of a drum attached to the wheel hub.

SUSPENSION

The suspension is responsible for providing greater driving comfort and protecting the vehicle's mechanics from ground vibrations, holes and unevenness. It is basically formed by a set of cylinders, springs and stops that absorb and soften the impacts on the wheels.
